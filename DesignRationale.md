**Design Rationale — The Dental Paradise Mobile App**

**Why This Business :**
I picked a dental clinic because it is one of those businesses where people genuinely feel anxious before even stepping in. I thought if the app itself feels calm, clean and easy to use it could actually reduce that anxiety a little before the patient even arrives. That became the core idea I kept coming back to while designing every screen.

**Color Choices :**
I went with Sky Blue, Deep Navy and Warm Amber as the main palette. Sky Blue felt right for a healthcare app because it is clean and trustworthy without being cold. Deep Navy gives the text and headings a strong, professional feel. Amber was the most thought out choice honestly. I needed a button color that felt warm and inviting rather than urgent or alarming because the last thing a nervous dental patient needs is a red or neon button telling them to book. Amber hits that balance well. Mint Green was used for success states and availability badges because it naturally reads as healthy and fresh which felt right for a dental context.

**Font Choices :**
I tried a few combinations and settled on these two. Lora for headings because it is a serif font that feels credible and professional, the kind of font you would expect from a trusted clinic. DM Sans for all body text and UI labels because it is clean, modern and very easy to read on a small screen.

**Onboarding and Login Screen :**
The first thing I thought about was how to make the brand feel real from the very first screen. So I designed a custom tooth logo with soft outer rings to give it depth and personality. The tagline Your Smile Our Priority sits right below it so the visitor immediately understands what the app is for. The login form is kept minimal with just email, password and social login options so there is no friction getting in.

**Home Dashboard :**
The home screen had to do a lot without feeling cluttered. I added an upcoming appointment banner at the top so the most important information is always visible first. Below that is a search bar, then services and doctors. The live appointments section at the bottom updates automatically after every booking which makes the app feel like it is actually working and tracking things for the user rather than just being a form.

**Specialist Selection Screen :**
I added filter chips at the top so users can quickly narrow down by specialty. Each doctor card shows the name, specialty, star rating and an availability badge. I kept the Book button right on the card itself so the user does not have to go into a separate profile just to take action. The fewer taps to book the better.

**Booking Screen :**
I wanted the booking experience to feel as smooth as possible. The weekly calendar strip lets you scroll through dates without any confusion. Time slots are shown as chips with three clear states, available, selected and unavailable so you instantly know what you can and cannot pick. The doctor summary card at the top of this screen was important because it reminds the user who they are booking with so there is no second guessing.

**Confirmation Screen :**
I added a pulsing mint green checkmark animation at the top because after going through the whole booking flow the user deserves a moment that feels satisfying and reassuring. The booking summary below it shows all the details clearly so the patient knows exactly what they have booked and when to show up.

**What I Learnt :**
This project taught me that designing for healthcare is very different from designing for something like a saloon or an ecommerce site. Every color, every font and every interaction has to make the user feel safe and confident. I also learned that the technical side of connecting screens with real dynamic data is just as important as how things look. Getting the doctor routing and the live appointments update to work correctly taught me more about UX thinking than any visual decision I made.
